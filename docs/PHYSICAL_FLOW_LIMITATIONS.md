# Physical Flow Limitations

This document describes what OpenLanePhysicalBench (Phase 10) does and does not
guarantee. These limitations apply to all physical-flow readiness checks
including config validation, report parsing, GDS hashing, and evidence pack
generation.

## Core Limitation

> Passing OpenLanePhysicalBench does not mean the design has been fabricated,
> accepted by Tiny Tapeout, timing-closed, power-characterised, physically
> tested, certified, or proven safe for real-world actuation. It means the
> generated artifacts passed structural and fixture-based report-parsing checks.
> Actual physical results require official OpenLane flow, foundry PDK,
> DRC/LVS signoff, and physical measurement.

## Fixture Reports, Not Real Flow

Unless the official OpenLane/OpenROAD flow was run on this system with a
qualified PDK:

- **DRC reports** are parsed from fixture files, not generated by a real
  physical verification run. A `DRC_CLEAN` status from a fixture report does
  not mean the layout is DRC-clean in any foundry process.
- **LVS reports** are parsed from fixture files. A `LVS_CLEAN` status does
  not mean layout matches schematic in any foundry-qualified environment.
- **Timing reports** are parsed from fixture files. A `TIMING_REPORT_PASS`
  status does not mean the design is timing-closed or meets any real clock
  frequency target.
- **Area stats** are parsed from fixture files. They do not represent
  real die area, utilisation, or gate-level measurements.

## GDS Hash Does Not Equal Tapeout-Ready GDS

- A `GDS_HASH_CREATED` status means a `.gds` file was found and its bytes
  were hashed with SHA-256. It does not mean the GDS is valid, DRC-clean,
  LVS-clean, or suitable for fabrication.
- A `GDS_MISSING` status means no `.gds` file was available. In this case,
  other artifacts (RTL, wrapper, config, pinout, reports) are hashed
  instead. This provides integrity verification, not fabrication readiness.
- No hash result — present or absent — should be interpreted as evidence of
  tapeout readiness.

## No Claim of Real Power, Real Area, Real Timing

OpenLanePhysicalBench does not measure, estimate, or claim:

- **Real power consumption** — No dynamic power, no leakage power, no thermal
  analysis, no IR drop analysis.
- **Real area** — No gate-level area after synthesis, no physical die area
  measurement, no utilisation analysis.
- **Real timing** — No static timing analysis signoff, no clock tree synthesis,
  no setup/hold verification, no clock domain crossing analysis.

All metrics are structural or fixture-based. They do not represent physical
measurements from a fabricated device or a signoff-quality EDA flow.

## No NVIDIA Comparison

OpenLanePhysicalBench does not compare to, benchmark against, or make any
claims relative to any NVIDIA product, tool, workflow, or design methodology.
No performance comparison to any commercial EDA tool or proprietary chip
design flow is implied or stated.

## No Medical, Defence, or Robotics Certification

OpenLanePhysicalBench is not certified, validated, or suitable as evidence
for any regulated application:

- **Medical devices** — Does not meet IEC 60601, FDA guidance, or medical
  device regulatory requirements. A `PHYSICAL_BENCH_PASS` result must never
  be used as evidence of medical device safety.
- **Defence and aerospace** — Does not meet DO-254, MIL-STD-882, or
  equivalent standards. Must not be used as a sole verification artifact
  for safety-critical avionics, weapons systems, or defence hardware.
- **Robotics and autonomous systems** — Does not validate real-time
  constraints, sensor fusion correctness, motion safety envelopes, or
  hardware/software interactions required for safe robotic operation.
- **Safety-critical deployment** — Must not be used as the primary means
  of demonstrating functional safety for any system where failure could
  result in personal injury, death, environmental damage, significant
  property damage, or loss of critical infrastructure.

## English-Only, Public-Safe Wording

All output from OpenLanePhysicalBench uses English-only, public-safe language.
The `public_wording` and `limitation` fields are included in every JSON
output and HTML report. These fields must not be omitted when results are
shared, published, or used for decision-making.

The mandatory wording is:

> OpenLanePhysicalBench checks whether a tiny DTL gate design can move
> from open-silicon preparation toward a reproducible ASIC physical-flow
> readiness review. It does not guarantee silicon correctness, fabrication
> readiness, timing signoff, real power, real area, physical durability,
> regulatory conformance or safety-critical deployment.

## Honest Claims

You can honestly claim:

- ChipGate ran structural and fixture-based report-parsing checks on the
  design artifacts.
- Artifact hashes are reproducible with SHA-256 verification.
- The design passed or failed specific structural readiness stages.
- Toolchain coverage was measured at a specific point in time on a specific
  system.
- Evidence packs contain integrity-verified hashes for reproducibility.

You cannot honestly claim:

- The design is ready for fabrication or tapeout.
- The design has been accepted by Tiny Tapeout.
- The design is timing-closed or power-characterised.
- The GDS is valid or DRC-clean in any foundry process.
- The design is certified for any safety-critical application.
- The design has been tested on real silicon or real hardware.
- The design is suitable for medical, defence, or robotics use.
- The results are comparable to any commercial EDA tool output.

## What Is Required for Real Physical Results

- Foundry-qualified PDK
- Complete timing analysis (STA) with signoff corners
- Power analysis (dynamic and leakage) with IR drop
- DRC and LVS signoff in the target foundry process
- Physical verification (RC extraction, antenna checks)
- Device qualification testing
- Regulatory compliance review (if applicable)
- Professional tapeout signoff
- Post-silicon validation and testing

OpenLanePhysicalBench provides none of these. It is a pre-silicon
structural readiness filter, not a replacement for professional EDA flows
or physical signoff.
